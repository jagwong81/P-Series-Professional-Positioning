Someone asked me a question not long ago:
“Were you a designer, a planner, or a project coordinator?”
My usual answer is:
“I was a project manager.”
But that answer often leads to another question.
Because many people's understanding of a project manager is quite different from what the role involved in the laboratory projects I worked on.
In those projects, project management often included laboratory planning, design coordination, user engagement, consultant coordination, construction interfaces, commissioning support, and project handover activities.
Many of these responsibilities were not separated into different roles. They were often part of the same position and the same project responsibility.
So while my title was Project Manager, a large portion of my work sat between disciplines, teams, and project phases.
Recently, I've been working toward re-entering the AEC industry here in Canada.
As I've been reflecting on my previous experience and thinking about where I want to continue building my career, I've found myself looking less at job titles and more at the actual work behind them.
If someone asked me the same question today, my answer would still begin with:
“I was a project manager.”
But moving forward, I see myself contributing most on the planning, coordination, and multidisciplinary collaboration side of project delivery.
Not because those responsibilities are new to me.
Because they have been part of my work for a very long time.
